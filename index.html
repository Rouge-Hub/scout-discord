Terms of Service

Last updated: July 15, 2026

By adding or using Scout in a Discord server, you agree to these Terms of Service. If you do not agree, do not use Scout and remove it from your server.

1. What Scout does

Scout provides Discord utility features such as slash commands, server information, user information, avatars, banners, dice rolls, coin flips, reminders, admin-controlled messages, and optional AI chat responses when configured by the bot owner.

2. Acceptable use

You may not use Scout to harass people, spam servers, impersonate others, break Discord's rules, distribute harmful content, or violate any law. Server owners and moderators are responsible for how Scout is used in their communities.

3. Availability

Scout is provided as-is. It may go offline, restart, lose temporary reminders, or stop responding without notice. Scout is not meant for emergency, safety-critical, financial, medical, or legal use.

4. AI responses

If AI chat is enabled, Scout may send messages and attachments to an external AI provider for processing. AI responses can be incomplete, incorrect, or inappropriate. You should not rely on them as professional advice.

5. Changes

These terms may be updated from time to time. Continued use of Scout after changes means you accept the updated terms.

6. Contact

For questions or removal requests, contact the bot owner through Discord: llrougell.

Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 15, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains what Scout may process when it is used in a Discord server.

1. Information Scout may process

Scout may process Discord user IDs, usernames, display names, server IDs, channel IDs, command inputs, message content that mentions Scout, image attachment URLs sent with a Scout mention, and temporary reminder text.

2. How information is used

Scout uses this information to respond to commands, display Discord account or server information, send reminders, continue short follow-up conversations, and provide optional AI responses when that feature is enabled.

3. Temporary memory

Scout keeps recent chat context in memory for about 10 minutes per user and channel while the bot is running. This memory is temporary and is cleared when it expires or when the bot restarts.

4. Third-party services

Scout runs on Discord and depends on Discord's platform. If AI chat is enabled, prompts, recent conversation context, and image attachment URLs may be sent to OpenRouter or the configured AI provider so Scout can generate a reply.

5. Data storage

This version of Scout does not include a database. It may log technical errors in the bot console for troubleshooting. Do not send private or sensitive information to Scout.

6. Your choices

You can stop a conversation by mentioning Scout and saying stop, end, done, cancel, never mind, or goodbye. Server administrators can remove Scout from a server at any time.

7. Contact

For privacy questions or removal requests, contact the bot owner through Discord: llrougell.
